# Kombucha Tea: A Functional Beverage and All its Aspects
**Source:** PMC / National Center for Biotechnology Information (NCBI)
**URL:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12103323/
**Date accessed:** March 2026

## Key Findings

- Peer-reviewed scientific article covering kombucha's composition, health benefits, and fermentation science
- Kombucha contains: organic acids, B vitamins, probiotics, polyphenols, and trace enzymes
- Fermentation by SCOBY (Symbiotic Culture of Bacteria and Yeast) produces acetic acid, glucuronic acid, and other bioactive compounds

## Health Benefits (Scientifically Supported)

- Antimicrobial properties (from acetic acid)
- Antioxidant activity (from tea polyphenols, especially black and green tea varieties)
- Gut microbiome support through probiotic activity
- Potential anti-inflammatory effects (under study)
- Hepatoprotective (liver-supporting) properties in animal studies

## Fermentation Notes

- Over 70% of the human immune system is associated with gut microbiota
- Kombucha supports microbiome diversity, which is increasingly linked to mental and physical health outcomes
- The "vinegary" taste comes from acetic acid; consumer education is key to overcoming taste objections

## Regulatory Relevance

- ABV must be monitored carefully during fermentation — EU regulations classify drinks above 0.5% ABV as alcoholic
- Live cultures complicate shelf life and cold-chain logistics; pasteurized vs. raw is a key product decision

## Relevance

Critical scientific background for health claims, product formulation, and regulatory compliance in Germany.
